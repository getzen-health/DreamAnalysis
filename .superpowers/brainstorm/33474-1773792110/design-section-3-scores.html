Design Section 3: Score Engines

6 scores that update in real-time as data flows through the pipeline. Plus our EEG advantage.

Recovery Score

0-100%

"How ready is your body to perform today?"

Inputs (weighted):
HRV vs 14-day baseline25%
Resting HR vs baseline20%
Sleep Score (previous night)25%
Skin Temperature deviation10%
Respiratory Rate vs baseline10%
SpO2 vs baseline10%
+ EEG Bonus: EEG stress index from previous evening session adjusts recovery ±5%
Requires 14-day calibration period. Each metric scored as % of personal baseline (above baseline = boost, below = penalty).

Sleep Score

0-100%

"How well did you sleep last night?"

Inputs (weighted):
Time Asleep vs Goal30%
Sleep Stage Balance25%
HR Dip During Sleep20%
Sleep Efficiency25%
+ EEG Bonus: If EEG worn overnight: real sleep staging (92.98% accuracy) replaces wearable estimates. Dream detection + lucidity score added.
Sleep Needed = Goal + Strain Adjustment + Sleep Debt (7-day weighted, spread over 4 days) + Efficiency Buffer
Sleep Debt = sum of (goal - actual) over past 7 days, weighted (recent nights count more)

Strain Score

0-100+

"How much stress has your body absorbed today?"

Components:
Active StrainFrom logged workouts
Passive StrainSteps, movement, non-workout HR
Algorithm: TRIMP (Training Impulse)
TRIMP = duration(min) × avg_HR_ratio × e^(1.92 × avg_HR_ratio)
HR_ratio = (exerciseHR - restHR) / (maxHR - restHR)

Logarithmic scaling: strain = k × ln(1 + TRIMP)
Harder to reach high scores. Zone 5 contributes exponentially more than Zone 1.
+ EEG Bonus: Cognitive strain from focus/concentration sessions adds to total strain (mental workload is real physiological stress)
Target Strain = personalized based on recovery score + historical strain pattern. Green = in target, orange = above.

Stress Score

0-100

"How stressed is your body right now?"

Inputs:
HRV trend (declining = stress)30%
HR elevation above resting25%
Respiratory rate elevation15%
Skin temp deviation10%
+ EEG (game changer): High-beta/beta ratio, frontal asymmetry, theta suppression → direct neural stress measurement. Weight: 20% when EEG active. Bevel/Whoop can NEVER do this.

Nutrition Score

1-100

"How well are you eating?"

Part 1: Food Quality (AHEI):
Positive: vegetables, fruits, whole grains, nuts, omega-3, healthy oils
Negative: red meat, processed meat, alcohol, excess sugar, excess sodium
Based on Harvard's Alternate Healthy Eating Index
Part 2: Glucose Impact (if CGM):
Per-meal glucose response curve. Time to peak, peak height, return to baseline. Lower spike = higher score.
+ EEG Bonus: Food-mood correlation. "Meals high in processed sugar correlate with 23% lower focus scores 2 hours later."

Energy Bank

0-100

"Your body's battery level right now."

Formula:
energy = recovery × (1 - strain/max_strain) × sleep_factor × stress_penalty
Behavior:
  • Starts at Recovery Score each morning
  • Depletes with strain throughout day
  • Stress accelerates depletion
  • Good nutrition slows depletion
  • Recharges during sleep
+ EEG Bonus: Flow states BOOST energy bank (productive effort feels energizing). High cognitive load WITHOUT flow drains it faster.

Cardio Load & Fitness Metrics

Cardio Load (TRIMP)

ATL = 7-day exponentially weighted TRIMP
CTL = 42-day (6 week) exponentially weighted TRIMP
TSB = CTL - ATL (training stress balance)

7 Statuses:
TSB > 15: Detraining
TSB 5-15: Maintaining
TSB -5 to 5: Productive
TSB -15 to -5: Peaking
TSB < -15: Fatigued/Overtraining

Heart Rate Recovery

HRR = peak HR - HR at +2 minutes

Benchmarks:
> 50 bpm: Excellent
40-50: Good
30-40: Average
< 30: Below average

Tracked per workout. Trend = fitness improving or declining.

VO2 Max (read-only)

Read from Apple Watch or Garmin.
Not computed — wearable's estimate is used.

Display:
Current value + trend over time
Age/sex percentile ranking
Correlation with recovery + cardio load

  • Recovery/HRV trends per phase

  • Custom habits (user-defined)
  • Mood slider (morphing animation)
  • Morning prompts
  • Habit-score correlations ("hydration improves your recovery by 12%")

Smart Alarm

  • Sleep Needed — wakes when goal reached
  • Smart — lightest sleep phase in window
  • Regular — fixed time
  • No Alarm — tracks time to fall asleep
  • Uses EEG sleep staging if worn (most accurate)
  • Falls back to wearable motion data
