A. Weight & Body Composition

Data Sources

  • Apple HealthKit — body mass, body fat %, lean mass, BMI, height
  • Google Health Connect — weight, body fat, height
  • Smart scales (via HealthKit) — Withings, Eufy, Renpho, etc.
  • Manual input — weight entry in app
  • Oura — weight if synced to Apple Health

  • Exercise minutes — from wearable activity detection
  • Heart rate zones — time in each of 5 zones per workout
  • Heart Rate Recovery — post-workout HR drop (2 min)
  • Garmin/Oura — activity data via OAuth sync

C. Heart Rate Zones & Cardio Metrics

5 Heart Rate Zones

Zone 1 Active Recovery (50-59% max HR)
Zone 2 Fat Burning (60-69%)
Zone 3 Cardiovascular (70-79%)
Zone 4 High Intensity (80-89%)
Zone 5 Maximum Effort (90-100%)
Max HR = 220 - age (configurable)

Cardio Load (TRIMP)

  • Acute Training Load (ATL) — past 7 days weighted sum
  • Chronic Training Load (CTL) — past 6 weeks rolling
  • Load Balance — ATL/CTL ratio
  • 7 Cardio Statuses:
    Calibrating Detraining Maintaining Productive Peaking Fatigued Overtraining
  • Heart Rate Recovery — HR drop from peak to +2 min

E. How It Feeds the Pipeline

// Workout completed (manual or wearable sync)
1. INSERT workout → PG trigger: compute workout strain (TRIMP)
2. INSERT workout strain into health_samples (metric: "strain")
3. Pipeline kicks in → aggregation → score recomputation
4. Strain Score updates → Energy Bank updates → dashboard updates
 
// Weight logged (scale sync or manual)
1. INSERT body_metrics → PG trigger: compute 7-day moving avg
2. Compare to goal → rate of change check
3. If rapid change (>2%/week) → INSERT trend_alert → push notification
4. Weight trend feeds into Recovery + Nutrition score adjustments
 
// EEG worn during workout
1. Workout links to eeg_session via eeg_session_id FK
2. Post-workout: correlate flow state peaks with HR zone peaks
3. Generate insight: "You hit flow state in Zone 3 — optimal cardio intensity"
