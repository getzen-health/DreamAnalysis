EEG Band Powers — Core Features
  • delta (0.5–4 Hz) Deep sleep & unconscious processing. Increases during N3 sleep, high delta → you're crashing. Used by sleep staging model (92.98% acc). Also inverted proxy for arousal (low delta = more awake). Live
  • theta (4–8 Hz) Meditation, creativity, memory encoding, drowsiness. Frontal midline theta (FMT) from this band is the most reference-robust valence signal. Theta/beta ratio → cognitive load & creativity state. Live
  • alpha (8–12 Hz) Core emotion signal. Used in FAA (left vs right alpha → valence), alpha/beta ratio (relaxation), alpha suppression → task engagement. Primary frequency band for the entire emotion pipeline. Live
  • beta (12–30 Hz) Active thinking, concentration, anxiety, stress. High-beta (20–30 Hz) specifically = anxiety/fight-flight. Beta/(alpha+beta) → arousal. Most visible live change when user is stressed. Live
  • high_beta (20–30 Hz) Anxiety fraction of beta. Separated out because low-beta (focus) and high-beta (anxiety) have opposite connotations. high_beta/beta = anxiety fraction — key input to stress detector and food stress_eating state. Live
  • gamma (30–100 Hz) On Muse 2: mostly jaw-clenching EMG, not neural signal. Kept for completeness and lucid dream detection (40 Hz gamma burst = REM awareness). Explicitly zeroed in arousal/stress formulas after bug fix Feb 2026. ⚠ EMG Noise
Asymmetry Features — Valence Pipeline
  • FAA
    (frontal_asymmetry)     ln(AF8_alpha) − ln(AF7_alpha). Davidson (1992) — 30 years of replication. Left-frontal activation = approach/positive affect; right = withdrawal/negative. Blended 50/50 with alpha/beta ratio for final valence. Test-retest r=0.53–0.66 (40% noise per epoch). Live
  • DASM
    (5 features)     DE_AF8 − DE_AF7 per band (delta, theta, alpha, beta, gamma). Extends FAA logic to ALL bands. Differential Entropy version is more sensitive to phase-locked activity than raw power asymmetry. Published as optimal compact feature for 4-channel EEG. Live
  • RASM
    (5 features)     DE_AF8 / DE_AF7 per band. Ratio version of DASM — scale-invariant, less sensitive to absolute power drift across sessions. Paired with DASM in the 85-feature vector. Live
  • FMT
    (frontal_midline_theta)     Theta power at AF7 from ACC/mPFC. Less sensitive to reference electrode choice than FAA. Pleasant stimuli → higher left theta. Used as valence complement when FAA is unreliable. Returned in every prediction response as frontal_midline_theta. Live
  • temporal_asymmetry TP9 vs TP10 asymmetry. Temporal channels capture emotion-related activity in auditory and association cortex. Partially implemented — TP9/TP10 signals are unreliable on Muse 2 (2024 validation: non-physiological in 17/N recordings even with green indicators). ⚠ Unreliable
Derived Ratios — Why Each One
  • alpha_beta_ratio Relaxation vs alertness. Core input to valence (tanh blend). High = relaxed, low = alert/stressed. Used in 3 separate code paths → the primary redundancy target. Live
  • theta_beta_ratio Creativity / drowsiness vs focus. High theta/beta = meditative or drowsy state. Input to cognitive load, flow state, and drowsiness detectors. Research basis: Schomer & Lopes da Silva (2011). Live
  • alpha_theta_ratio Calm wakefulness vs drowsiness. High = relaxed-but-awake (Zen state). Drops during both high stress and sleepiness. Used by meditation classifier for depth estimation. Live
  • high_beta_frac Anxiety fraction of total beta. high_beta/beta. Separates anxious focus from calm focus. Direct input to stress_index formula and food stress_eating state biomarker. Live
  • delta_theta_ratio Deep sleep marker. Very high delta/theta = N3 sleep. Used exclusively by sleep staging model. Not relevant during wake sessions. Live
Hjorth Parameters + Spectral Features
  • hjorth_activity Signal variance (power proxy). Faster to compute than Welch PSD. Used in artifact detection — sudden activity spike = blink or electrode pop. Live
  • hjorth_mobility Mean frequency of signal. Ratio of derivative variance to signal variance. Increases with beta/gamma activity. Useful for gross state changes without full PSD. Live
  • hjorth_complexity Signal "roughness" — how different from a sine wave. High complexity = many frequency components active simultaneously. Low during deep sleep (delta dominates), high during cognition. Live
  • spectral_entropy How "spread out" power is across frequencies. Low entropy = one frequency dominates (e.g., alpha peak in eyes-closed relaxation). High = broadband activity (cognition or noise). One per epoch. Live
  • differential_entropy
    (per band)     Information content of the band's power distribution. More sensitive than raw power to phase-locked emotional activity. Used in DASM/RASM features (above). Published as the single best feature for EEG emotion recognition (Shi et al., 2013). Live
Derived Emotion Indices — What the App Shows You
  • valence
    (−1 to +1)     Negative ↔ positive feeling. 50% FAA + 50% alpha/beta ratio (both normalized via tanh). Russell's circumplex X-axis. Published cross-subject accuracy: 60–72%. Individual variation: ±0.25 per epoch is noise. Derived
  • arousal
    (0 to 1)     Calm ↔ energetic. 45% beta/(beta+alpha) + 30% (1 − alpha fraction) + 25% (1 − delta proxy). Russell's circumplex Y-axis. More reliable than valence (~10 points better). Gamma removed after EMG bug fix. Derived
  • stress_index
    (0 to 1)     Physiological stress level. 45% high_beta/(beta+alpha) + 30% (theta/alpha)×0.3 + 25% high_beta_frac. Validated against HRV and cortisol in Al-Shargie et al. (2016). Derived
  • focus_index
    (0 to 1)     Sustained attention level. 45% beta_ratio + 40% (1 − alpha_ratio) + 15% (1 − theta_beta). High beta + suppressed alpha = focused task engagement. Gamma removed (was detecting jaw clenching as "focus"). Derived
  • relaxation_index
    (0 to 1)     Relaxation state. Primarily alpha dominance + low beta. Inverse of stress_index. Shown on the biofeedback breathing page to give real-time feedback during 4-7-8 breathing exercises. Derived
  • confidence
    (0 to 1)     Model confidence score. Calibrated from prediction probability. Shown as a badge on the emotion label. Below 0.4 → displays "uncertain" to prevent misleading readings during artifact epochs. Derived
Food-Emotion Biomarkers — The Novel Module
  • craving_carbs High FAA + high beta. Positive valence + arousal → anticipatory reward state. Dopaminergic activation preceding intake. FAA weight 0.35, high_beta 0.40. Heuristic
  • appetite_suppressed Negative FAA + elevated delta. Right-frontal dominance + slow wave activity → withdrawal state. Consistent with anhedonic, post-stress appetite loss. FAA weight −0.20, delta 0.40. Heuristic
  • comfort_seeking Negative FAA + moderate theta. Negative valence + emotional-processing theta → reaching for food to regulate mood. Cross-cultural phenomenon (Ayurveda → sazón). FAA weight −0.40. Heuristic
  • balanced Neutral FAA + low beta + elevated theta/delta. Calm, not seeking reward, not avoiding — the homeostatic baseline eating state. FAA near zero, low high_beta. Heuristic
  • stress_eating Negative FAA + very high beta. Negative valence + hyperarousal → using food to dampen cortisol response. High_beta weight 0.50 (dominant signal). Most clinically relevant state. Heuristic
  • mindful_eating Slightly positive FAA + high theta. Mild positive valence + meditative theta → intentional, aware eating. Theta weight 0.40 (highest of any state). Correlates with slow eating, taste focus. Heuristic
⚠ All 6 states are theory-derived heuristics — no training data yet. Biomarker weights are physiologically motivated but unvalidated. This is the most novel (and most speculative) module. The 30-day study is Phase 1 of validating these biomarkers.
Dream Parameters — What Gets Stored
  • dreamValence Emotional tone of dream content. GPT-5 extracts valence from free-text dream journal entries. −1 = nightmare, +1 = positive dream. The primary outcome variable for the continuity hypothesis test. Live
  • lucidityScore Self-reported lucidity 0–10. Cross-validated with overnight EEG gamma bursts during REM (Voss et al., 2009). Higher lucidity → more gamma at 40 Hz during detected REM periods. Live
  • nightmareFlag Binary nightmare indicator. Stored separately from valence because nightmare threshold is subjective. Used to flag distress and show mental health resources (welfare gate check). Live
  • symbols (JSON array) AI-extracted recurring imagery. GPT-5 identifies symbols (water, falling, flying, family, etc.) for pattern analysis across 30 days. Primary input to dream-patterns page trend chart. Live
  • noRecall Dream recall failure flag. ~55% of mornings. Critical to not confuse "forgot dream" with "no dream." Excluded from continuity analysis when true — treated as missing data, not negative valence. Live
  • welfareScore Morning wellbeing 1–10. Research welfare gate. Below threshold (≤2) → app shows mental health resources and flags session for researcher review. Ethical safety check built into the study protocol. Live
Health Metrics — What Comes From Outside EEG
  • heartRate BPM from Apple Health / Google Fit. Correlates with stress and arousal. Used as ground-truth cross-validation for EEG stress_index (if HR spikes and stress_index doesn't → model miss). Live
  • stressLevel Composite from health integrations. Populated from Apple Watch stress estimation or HRV-derived stress. Shown on main dashboard as a sanity check against EEG-derived stress_index. Live
  • sleepQuality Subjective 1–9 + Oura/Apple Sleep score. Dream recall correlates with sleep quality. Higher quality → more REM → better recall. Confound variable that must be controlled in the emotion→dream correlation. Live
  • dailySteps Activity level proxy. Exercise affects EEG (post-exercise alpha increase, better sleep architecture). Steps → covariate in the emotion→dream correlation model. Prevents confounding "had a great dream because I ran 10K". Live
  • sleepDuration Total hours asleep. REM proportion increases with sleep length (REM rebound). Short night → less dreaming → confounded noRecall. Used to weight dream data quality in the continuity analysis. Live
Signal Pipeline Parameters
  • epoch_ready Minimum 4 seconds buffered. False in first 4 seconds. Prevents classifying on too few samples where Welch PSD is unreliable (theta at 4 Hz needs ≥2 complete cycles = ≥500ms minimum; 4 sec gives 16+ cycles). Live
  • signalQuality (SQI) 1–5 scale, 5-point composite. Gating: below 40% → frozen EMA, no new prediction. Prevents artifact epochs from corrupting emotion timeline. Note: HSI (horseshoe indicator from Muse) is unreliable — SQI is recomputed from signal. Live
  • baseline_mean / std Per-user resting state calibration. Record 2 min eyes-closed → compute mean/std per feature → z-score normalize all subsequent live features. Gives +15–29% accuracy. Must be re-recorded every session (drifts across days). Live
  • mastoid_ref (TP9 + TP10) / 2. Re-reference offline to remove Fpz contamination from Muse default reference. Applied in brainflow_manager before data enters Python. Critical for meaningful FAA values. Live
  • amplitude_threshold (75µV) Artifact rejection gate. Any epoch exceeding ±75µV is rejected — EMA frozen, model not updated. Catches blinks (delta spike at Fp channels), muscle burst, electrode pop. Paper-validated threshold (Krigolson 2021). Live
⚠ Redundancy Report

10 Issues Found

1. Band Powers Recomputed 8× Per Frame
extract_band_powers() runs Welch PSD independently in emotion_classifier, food_emotion_predictor, sleep_staging, flow_state, stress_detector, and 3 others. Fix: compute once in /analyze-eeg, pass vector to all 16 models.
2. Arousal Formula in 3 Places
Computed in _predict_features(), _predict_onnx() (twice — duplicate lines 509+512), _predict_sklearn(). Slightly different formulas across paths. Fix: single compute_arousal() utility.
3. Valence Formula Inconsistent
ONNX and sklearn paths use only alpha/beta ratio. Feature path uses 50% FAA + 50% alpha/beta. Three different outputs for the same emotional state depending on which model loads.
4. Signal Quality Checked 3 Ways
signal_quality.py (40% gate), artifact_detector.py (100µV), emotion_classifier.py inline (75µV). Three different amplitude thresholds in the same pipeline.
5. EMA Smoothing Duplicated
emotion_classifier._ema_probs (α=0.35) and state_transitions.py EMA (different α). Then client-side React applies a third pass of smoothing.
6. Neurofeedback Thresholds in 3 Files
Hardcoded in protocol_engine.py, rl_environment.py, and the /neurofeedback/start endpoint. Diverged over time.
7. Emotion → Label in 3 Places
_raw_to_discrete_emotion() in classifier, inline in emotion_shift_detector.py, and client-side TypeScript. Same logic, different thresholds.
8. 5 Model-Loading Paths in emotion_classifier
mega_lgbm → ONNX → sklearn → features → fallback. Each path reinitializes scaler/PCA objects. Memory leak risk on repeated calls.
9. temporal_asymmetry Half-Implemented
TP9/TP10 asymmetry computed but inconsistently included. Sometimes used in RASM, sometimes not. 2024 paper: TP9/TP10 signals non-physiological in 17/N recordings.
10. stressLevel in DB + stress_index in ML
Two separate stress values: healthMetrics.stressLevel (from Apple Health) and ML stress_index. No reconciliation logic. Dashboard shows them in separate cards with no explanation of the difference.

The Core Logic

Why these 3 domains together?

The Emotional Transfer Chain hypothesis:

  • Emotion (EEG) — daytime valence/arousal state shapes what gets consolidated during sleep
  • Food (eating states) — food choices are both a proxy for emotional state AND a mediating variable (blood sugar, gut-brain axis) affecting dream content
  • Dreams (journal + AI) — the downstream readout of what the brain processed emotionally that day (Continuity Hypothesis, Domhoff 2003)
✓ The three domains are genuinely mechanistically linked — not random features.
Missing Parameters

Gaps in the pipeline

  • Per-user personalization — population-average thresholds only. Within-subject vs cross-subject gap: 26 points accuracy.
  • HRV — Muse 2 has PPG sensor (board preset "p50") but it's not being collected. Best stress ground-truth available on-device.
  • Sleep spindles / K-complexes — code exists in eeg_processor.py, not wired into sleep staging output.
  • Cortisol proxy — high-beta pattern in first 30 min post-waking = cortisol awakening response. Not captured.
  • Epoch length 4s — research recommends 8s for valence. Sliding window exists but window size is a tunable parameter not yet optimized.
