## 1. YOUR SITUATION: INDEPENDENT RESEARCHER IN THE USA

As an independent researcher without university or hospital affiliation, you cannot use an institutional IRB (which serves only affiliated researchers). You need a **commercial (independent) IRB**, sometimes called a "for-profit IRB" or "central IRB."

**Good news:** Commercial IRBs routinely work with independent researchers, industry sponsors, and small research groups. The process is straightforward for minimal-risk studies like yours.

**Relevant regulation:** 45 CFR 46 (the "Common Rule") is the US federal regulation for human subjects research. It applies to federally funded research, but **most journals require IRB approval regardless of funding source** — so getting commercial IRB approval is essential for publishing.

## 2. YOUR STUDY'S REVIEW CATEGORY

Your study qualifies for **Expedited Review**, not Full Board Review. This is important — expedited review is:
- Faster (days to weeks vs. months)
- Cheaper
- Reviewed by a single IRB member rather than convened full board

**Basis for expedited review (45 CFR 46.110):**

| Category | Your Study |
|----------|-----------|
| **Category 6** | Collection of data through noninvasive procedures routinely employed in clinical practice — EEG is non-invasive, no electrical stimulation | ✓ |
| **Category 7** | Research on individual or group characteristics or behavior — structured questionnaire, interviews, or surveys | ✓ |

**Why it's minimal risk:**
- No electrical stimulation or currents applied to participants
- No blood draws, injections, or invasive procedures
- Consumer device (Muse 2) is commercially available, CE/FCC cleared
- Questions about daily life, not trauma, abuse, or stigmatizing topics
- No deception
- No special vulnerable populations targeted: children (under 18 excluded), prisoners (not targeted). Pregnant women are not excluded — the study presents no known risk to them (passive consumer device, no electrical stimulation, non-invasive questionnaire). Your IRB application should explicitly note this and confirm that Subpart B of 45 CFR 46 has been considered; most IRBs will agree it does not impose additional requirements given the minimal-risk nature of this study.

For a minimal-risk, expedited-review pilot study like yours with N=20–30 participants:

| Item | Estimated Cost |
|------|---------------|
| Initial review fee (expedited) | $1,500–$3,500 |
| Annual continuing review (Year 2 if study spans two years) | $500–$1,500 |
| Amendment fee (if you modify protocol) | $300–$800 per amendment |
| Expedited review surcharge (if you need faster turnaround) | $0–$500 |
| **Total (one-year study, no amendments)** | **$1,500–$4,000** |

**Tips to reduce costs:**
- Submit a complete, clean application the first time — revision requests add time and sometimes cost
- Ask each IRB if they have any "new investigator," "independent researcher," or "academic" pricing discounts
- Quorum typically has the lowest fees for small independent studies
- Some IRBs charge per protocol, not per site — confirm this if you may add collection sites later

## 6. STEP-BY-STEP APPLICATION PROCESS

### Step 1 — Complete CITI Training (FREE, required)
Register at https://www.citiprogram.org (free for independent researchers).
Complete:
- **Social/Behavioral Research Investigators** course (Modules 1–10, ~6 hours)
- Optional but recommended: **Good Clinical Practice (GCP) — Social/Behavioral Research**

Print/download your completion certificate. This is required by all commercial IRBs.

### Step 2 — Request a Quote
Email your top 2–3 IRB choices with this brief message:

We anticipate this qualifies for expedited review under 45 CFR 46.110, Categories 6 and 7.

### Step 3 — Choose Your IRB and Create an Account
Select based on cost, turnaround time, and responsiveness to your quote request. Create a researcher account on their online submission portal.

---

### Step 4 — Complete the IRB Application Form
Each IRB has their own form (usually fillable PDF or web form). It will ask:
- Principal investigator information (name, contact, credentials)
- Study title and brief description
- Number of participants
- Location(s) of data collection
- Funding source (none — independent)
- Study duration
- Consent process
- Risk level justification
- Data security measures

Use your protocol documents to fill in the answers — you've already written most of this content.

---

### Step 5 — Assemble and Submit the Full Package

Upload all documents (convert .md files to PDF first using pandoc or a Markdown-to-PDF converter):
```bash
# Convert all IRB documents to PDF (requires pandoc + a PDF engine like wkhtmltopdf)
pandoc 01_STUDY_PROTOCOL.md -o 01_STUDY_PROTOCOL.pdf
pandoc 02_INFORMED_CONSENT_FORM.md -o 02_INFORMED_CONSENT_FORM.pdf
pandoc 03_RECRUITMENT_MATERIALS.md -o 03_RECRUITMENT_MATERIALS.pdf
pandoc 04_DATA_SECURITY_PLAN.md -o 04_DATA_SECURITY_PLAN.pdf
```

Pay the application fee (most IRBs accept credit card or ACH bank transfer).

---

### Step 6 — Respond to IRB Queries (if any)
The reviewer may ask clarifying questions or request minor modifications. Respond promptly and thoroughly. For expedited review, queries usually arrive within 5–10 business days of submission.

Common query topics for studies like yours:
- Request to add specific language to the consent form about data sharing
- Clarification on whether audio/video is recorded during sessions
- Request for the validated scales (SAM/PANAS) themselves as attachments

---

### Step 7 — Receive Approval Letter
The IRB will issue a formal **Approval Letter** (sometimes called a "Determination Letter"). This will include:
- IRB Protocol Number
- Approval date
- Expiration date (usually 1 year from approval — requires continuing review)
- List of approved documents with version numbers and dates
- Any conditions or stipulations

**Save this letter.** It is required for journal submission and should be referenced in any publication.

---

### Step 8 — Begin Data Collection
You may NOT collect any data until you receive the written approval letter. Do not begin data collection based on verbal assurances.

---

### Step 9 — Annual Continuing Review
Before the expiration date on your approval letter, submit a continuing review report to the IRB. This typically involves:
- Status update (how many participants enrolled, any adverse events)
- Updated documents if any changes were made
- Renewal fee ($500–$1,500)

## 7. CITI TRAINING DETAILS

**CITI Program** (Collaborative Institutional Training Initiative) is the standard human subjects training platform accepted by all US IRBs.

- **Website:** https://www.citiprogram.org
- **Cost:** Free for independent researchers (basic course)
- **Time:** 6–8 hours (can be done over multiple sittings)
- **Certificate validity:** Most IRBs accept certificates up to 3 years old; some commercial IRBs require more recent training (within 1–2 years). **Confirm the specific validity period with your chosen IRB before submitting** — do not assume 3 years applies universally.

**Required courses for this study:**
1. Social/Behavioral Research Investigators (Basic Course) — REQUIRED
2. Responsible Conduct of Research (RCR) — recommended
3. Information Privacy and Security — recommended (given your data security plan)

**How to register:**
1. Go to https://www.citiprogram.org
2. Click "Register" → "I am an Independent Researcher (not affiliated with a CITI member institution)"
3. Select your courses
4. Complete all modules
5. Download completion certificate as PDF

## 9. DURING AND AFTER THE STUDY — IRB OBLIGATIONS

Once approved, you have ongoing obligations:

| Obligation | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Report Adverse Events** | Promptly report any unexpected harm or discomfort experienced by participants (use the IRB's AE reporting form) |
| **Report Unanticipated Problems** | Report any unexpected events that increase risk or affect participant rights |
| **Submit Amendments** | Get IRB approval BEFORE making any changes to the protocol, consent form, or recruitment materials — do not just modify documents unilaterally |
| **Annual Continuing Review** | Submit a progress report before the approval expiration date each year |
| **Study Closure Report** | When you finish, notify the IRB that the study is closed |
| **Record Retention** | Keep all IRB correspondence, approval letters, and signed consent forms for at least 7 years |
